02 — GLP-1

Semaglutide (GLP-1) Program

A medically supervised GLP-1 program designed to reduce appetite, improve metabolic health, and support long-term weight management. Gradual, consistent weight loss without extreme dieting.

What Is Semaglutide?

A GLP-1 receptor agonist that regulates appetite, blood sugar, and metabolism by slowing digestion, increasing fullness, and reducing hunger signals from the brain. One of the most effective non-surgical weight loss tools available.

How It Supports Weight Loss

  • Reduces appetite and food cravings significantly
  • Increases satiety after meals
  • Stabilizes blood sugar levels
  • Supports insulin sensitivity
  • Helps patients maintain calorie control naturally

Administration

  • Subcutaneous injection once weekly
  • Fine insulin-style syringe — easy to self-administer at home
  • Gradual dose titration for comfort and safety
  • Ongoing monitoring and adjustments by our medical team

03 — Dual Action

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) Program

A medically supervised dual-action program designed to enhance fat loss, appetite control, and metabolic efficiency. A powerful option for patients with significant metabolic resistance.

What Is Tirzepatide?

A dual GLP-1 and GIP receptor agonist targeting multiple metabolic pathways. By activating two hormone receptors instead of one, Tirzepatide may offer enhanced weight loss for patients needing stronger metabolic support.

How It Works

  • Reduces appetite and cravings through dual receptor activation
  • Enhances insulin sensitivity
  • Improves blood sugar control
  • Increases feelings of fullness after meals
  • Supports sustained fat loss over time

Who May Benefit

  • Have insulin resistance or metabolic syndrome
  • Need stronger appetite and glucose control

Not necessarily. Many patients transition to a maintenance plan after achieving their goals, with ongoing lifestyle support.

Is it safe for non-diabetics?

Yes. These medications are approved and prescribed for weight loss in eligible patients without diabetes, under physician supervision.

Which medication is best for me?

Your provider will recommend the best option based on your health history, goals, and metabolic needs during your consultation.

Are there side effects?

Possible side effects include mild nausea or digestive changes, which often improve over time. We carefully monitor every patient throughout treatment.
